abstract

If you had to imagine the perfect plate of food, delivered to you from anywhere in the world in mere seconds, what would it be? Would you fixate on the taste? Would plating come to mind? Or would your mind crave a piper platter of nostalgia and comfort?

Here’s another exercise. Think back to your last plated meal. How long did it take for you to plate your food? If you’re anything like me, it probably took seconds to plate if not quicker.

Most daily habits associated with meals, like setting the table or arranging food on a plate, are instinctual and practical. Most people abhor the idea of wasting intellectual energy a seemingly trivial task. Because, who notices if a dumpling sits at a 45 degree angle 5 centimeters from the plate’s center? Lest we ignore the food influencers, the answer is practically no one.

But if you look outside the home, plating is a highly desirable virtue of any great meal. Just look at restaurants, culinary schools and traditional dinner parties. Chefs devote months to perfecting a seasonal menu with presentations inspired by art, nature, and tradition. Culinary students spend weeks training their eyes on recognizing aesthetics in plating traditions from around the globe. Online food photography, a more accessible genre nowadays, offers another model to plate delectable meals.

It almost seems reductive to say "You know good plating when you see it" or "it's a skill you're born with." But that is impossible — people must learn the rules for plating from someone, or somewhere. One answer lies in oral traditions: plating traditions are passed down from teacher to student and from elder to younger generation. With enough repetition, students adopt these plating traditions and make these practices their own. Moreover, ideas of beautiful food photography can come from the visually rich and natural environment, television shows and the internet including blogs, advertisements, and Instagram. With all these competing sources for food plating, is it true that the perfect plate of food lies beyond our grasp?

The easy thing would be to accept the facts and call it a day. But after researching this topic for hours, I noticed certain themes about plating that could be codified. Conflicting advice be damned -- could a computer, trained only on rules for beautiful and traditional platings and a hand picked dataset, pleasingly arrange food?

Actually, it can! The quality, on the other hand, depends on the quality of the input. The more images, the better the computer can comprehend plating. And the more measurements, the more accurate the final food placement. Hours of research and hundreds of lines of code later, this comprehensive primer on food plating brings us one step closer to formalizing the definition of an “aesthetically pleasing plate of food.” So if humans can learn and internalize rules, surely a computer can follow a similar procedure, or at the very least try to.

My thesis research is three fold. First, I cover the history of plating, starting from its earliest traditions and rituals to its modern adoption into the history of food photography and restaurants. Then, I stitch together interviews with chefs, culinary instructors and stylists to showcase present day attitudes towards plating. Finally, I propose and implement two methods to achieve balanced and aesthetically pleasing plates of food.
